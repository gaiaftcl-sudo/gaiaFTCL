## Core systems (citations)

### *Nerearchaeum marumarumayae* and *Stromatodesulfovibrio nilemahensis*

Recent **cryo-electron tomography** and community characterization of Shark Bay stromatolite mats describe an Asgard-group archaeon **Nerearchaeum marumarumayae** interacting with the sulfate-reducing bacterium ***Stromatodesulfovibrio nilemahensis***. The archaeon donates small organics / reductant; the bacterium returns biosynthetic currency (e.g. amino acids, vitamins). **Intercellular nanotubes** assembled in the bacterial partner provide a **hardware-level** route for molecular exchange—an observable transport layer prior to wholesale systemic merger.

**Primary reference (preprint / lineage):** R. et al., *An Asgard archaeon from a modern analog of ancient microbial mats* — bioRxiv **2025.07.22.663070** (DOI `10.1101/2025.07.22.663070`). *Use the peer-reviewed version when available and cite that DOI in production docs.*

### Nanotubes in Archaea (review context)

Extracellular vesicles and nanotube-like structures in archaea are surveyed in the **FEMS Microbiology Letters** microlife review: *Extracellular membrane vesicles and nanotubes in Archaea* — DOI **`10.1093/femsml/uqab007`**.

### Tunneling nanotubes (TNTs) in eukaryotes (distant analogue)

Mammalian **tunneling nanotubes** are membranous bridges for intercellular cargo; they illustrate **conserved physical transport** at multicellular scale—**not** identity with archaeal filaments, but useful pedagogy for “long-distance state transport.”

### Intracellular order / ESP-adjacent framing (hypothesis-heavy)

Reports on **Lokiactin** and **eukaryotic signature proteins** in Asgard lineages concern **cytoskeletal / internal routing** in uncultured archaea. Cite **primary isolate papers** when claiming structural detail; treat as **literature backdrop** for “routing modularity before nuclear enclosure,” not as substrate law.
